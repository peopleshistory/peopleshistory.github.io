In collaboration with Boston Review, we’ve been working on an exclusive side project to accompany the main podcast: A People’s Anthology. About Us

A People’s History is a documentary podcast about class struggle in the United States. Inspired by sections from Howard Zinn's landmark book, each six-episode season covers one local story, told from the viewpoint of common people.

Alejandro Ramirez is an associate editor of Nashville Scene, and grew up in Lawrence, Massachusetts. He is the former editor of Spare Change News in Boston. Conor Gillies grew up in Maine and studied history at Boston University. He produces for Radio Open Source.

Co-produced by Alison Bruzek, Rehanna Fernandes, Rosie Gillies, and Qainat Kahn. Editing help from Ben Shapiro, David Wallis, and Alissa Quart. Theme music by Marisa Anderson, original score by Liz Harris and Ilyas Ahmed. Major production help from Ed Paget, Patrick King, Cait Gillies, and Alex Silva.
